# Privacy Policy - Judge.ca

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** January 1, 2024

## 1. Introduction

Judge.ca Inc. ("we," "our," or "us") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our platform.

This policy complies with:
- Quebec's Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector
- Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA)
- Applicable international privacy laws

## 2. Information We Collect

### 2.1 Personal Information
We collect the following types of personal information:

**For All Users:**
- Name and contact information
- Email address and phone number
- Account credentials
- Profile information
- Communication records
- Payment information
- Device and usage data

**For Clients:**
- Legal needs and case descriptions
- Budget information
- Location data
- Communication preferences

**For Attorneys:**
- Professional credentials and bar numbers
- Professional experience and qualifications
- Professional liability insurance information
- Business address and contact information
- Professional biography and practice areas
- Availability and scheduling information

### 2.2 Technical Information
We automatically collect:
- IP addresses and device identifiers
- Browser type and version
- Operating system information
- Usage patterns and preferences
- Cookies and similar technologies
- Location data (with consent)

### 2.3 Communications
We may record and store:
- Messages between users and our support team
- Platform communications and interactions
- Customer service calls (with notice)

## 3. How We Collect Information

### 3.1 Direct Collection
- Account registration and profile creation
- Form submissions and surveys
- Customer service interactions
- Voluntary information sharing

### 3.2 Automatic Collection
- Cookies and tracking technologies
- Analytics tools and services
- Server logs and technical data
- Third-party integrations

### 3.3 Third-Party Sources
- Identity verification services
- Professional licensing databases
- Credit reporting agencies (for attorneys)
- Social media platforms (with consent)

## 4. How We Use Your Information

### 4.1 Primary Purposes
- **Matching Services:** Connect clients with appropriate attorneys
- **Account Management:** Maintain and service user accounts
- **Communication:** Facilitate platform communications
- **Payment Processing:** Handle financial transactions
- **Customer Support:** Provide assistance and resolve issues

### 4.2 Secondary Purposes
- **Platform Improvement:** Enhance features and functionality
- **Analytics:** Understand usage patterns and preferences
- **Marketing:** Send relevant communications (with consent)
- **Legal Compliance:** Meet regulatory requirements
- **Security:** Protect against fraud and abuse

### 4.3 Attorney-Specific Uses
- **Verification:** Confirm professional credentials
- **Profile Creation:** Display attorney information to clients
- **Performance Analytics:** Track matching success rates
- **Quality Assurance:** Monitor service quality

## 5. Information Sharing and Disclosure

### 5.1 Within the Platform
- Attorney profiles are visible to clients
- Client requests are shared with matched attorneys
- Reviews and ratings are publicly displayed
- Communication records may be accessible to relevant parties

### 5.2 Service Providers
We share information with:
- Payment processors and financial institutions
- Identity verification services
- Cloud hosting and storage providers
- Customer support platforms
- Analytics and marketing services

### 5.3 Legal Requirements
We may disclose information when required by:
- Court orders or legal process
- Law enforcement requests
- Regulatory investigations
- Protection of rights and safety
- Compliance with applicable laws

### 5.4 Business Transfers
Information may be transferred in connection with:
- Mergers or acquisitions
- Asset sales or transfers
- Corporate reorganizations
- Bankruptcy proceedings

## 6. Data Protection and Security

### 6.1 Security Measures
We implement:
- Industry-standard encryption
- Secure data transmission (SSL/TLS)
- Access controls and authentication
- Regular security assessments
- Employee training and background checks

### 6.2 Data Storage
- Data is stored in secure, Canadian-based facilities
- Regular backups and disaster recovery procedures
- Retention policies and secure disposal
- Compliance with data residency requirements

### 6.3 Attorney-Client Privilege
We do not:
- Access privileged attorney-client communications
- Interfere with professional confidentiality
- Claim ownership of privileged information
- Waive attorney-client privilege

## 10. Retention Periods

### 10.1 General Retention
We retain information for:
- **Account Data:** Duration of account plus 7 years
- **Communication Records:** 7 years from last contact
- **Payment Information:** As required by financial regulations
- **Legal Documents:** As required by law

### 10.2 Attorney Records
Attorney information is retained:
- Duration of professional relationship
- Minimum 7 years after account closure
- As required by professional regulations
- For legal and regulatory compliance
